# Google Ads Quality Score

## What Is Quality Score?

A 1-10 rating (10 = best) assigned to each keyword, estimating the quality and relevance of your ads, keywords, and landing pages. Higher Quality Score = lower CPCs and better ad positions.

## Three Components

### 1. Expected Click-Through Rate (CTR)
How likely your ad is to be clicked when shown. Based on historical performance of the keyword.

**Improve by:**
- Writing compelling ad copy with clear value propositions
- Using the keyword in headlines
- Including a strong call-to-action
- Testing multiple RSA headline variations
- Using ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets)

### 2. Ad Relevance
How closely your ad matches the intent behind the search query.

**Improve by:**
- Keeping ad groups tightly themed (one topic per ad group)
- Using keywords naturally in headlines and descriptions
- Matching ad messaging to search intent (informational vs transactional)
- Creating specific ads for each ad group (don't reuse generic ads)

### 3. Landing Page Experience
How relevant, useful, and easy-to-navigate your landing page is for users who click.

**Improve by:**
- Matching landing page content to ad messaging and keywords
- Fast page load speed (Core Web Vitals — LCP < 2.5s)
- Mobile-friendly design
- Clear, relevant content above the fold
- Easy navigation and clear CTA
- Original, useful content (not thin or duplicate)
- HTTPS / SSL certificate
- No intrusive interstitials

## Quality Score Impact

| Quality Score | CPC Impact | Ad Position |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| 10 | Up to 50% discount | Top positions |
| 7-9 | Below average CPC | Good positions |
| 5-6 | Average CPC | Average positions |
| 3-4 | Above average CPC | Lower positions |
| 1-2 | Significantly higher CPC | May not show |

## Quality Score vs Ad Rank

**Ad Rank** = Bid × Quality Score × Expected impact of extensions

Quality Score is one input; you can't buy your way to top positions with a low Quality Score. Conversely, a high Quality Score lets you pay less for the same position.

## Diagnostic Approach

When a keyword has a low Quality Score:

1. Check which component is "Below average":
   - Below average CTR → improve ad copy and extensions
   - Below average ad relevance → tighten ad group theming
   - Below average landing page → improve page speed and content relevance
2. Focus on the weakest component first
3. Allow 2-4 weeks for changes to affect Quality Score
4. Don't chase 10/10 — anything 7+ is good; focus your time on low-QS, high-volume keywords

## Common Mistakes

- Putting too many unrelated keywords in one ad group (kills ad relevance)
- Sending all traffic to the homepage instead of relevant landing pages
- Ignoring mobile experience
- Not using ad extensions (easy CTR boost)
- Pausing and reactivating keywords frequently (loses historical data)
